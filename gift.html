Sto. Niño Orphanage Gift-Giving


Date: October 22, 2025 | 02:30PM

Organizer: College of Computer Studies

Event Type: Community

Points: [1] Participant

What

So What

Now What

The Sto. Niño Orphanage Gift-Giving happened on October 22, 2025, at 2:30 PM. It was an activity for one of our subjects where we donated food, hygiene essentials, and other basic needs like diapers, laundry stuff, and tissues. We also made cards with short messages for the kids to make it a bit more personal.

Even though it was a simple activity, it felt good to do something for others. I didn’t expect much at first, but seeing how something small could make a difference really hit me. We didn’t get to stay long, but just knowing we helped was enough. I liked that it wasn’t just about giving material things — it was about showing care. The hardest part was probably just organizing everything, but once it was done, it felt nice to be part of it.

This event reminded me that kindness doesn’t have to be loud or huge to matter. It made me more aware of how important compassion is, even in small acts. I can apply that by being more thoughtful in everyday life, not just during school projects. If I could do it again, I’d want to actually spend more time with the kids and interact with them.

Because of this activity, I am more aware of the value of simple kindness and generosity.
