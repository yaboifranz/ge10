The Unseen Flame


Date: September 27, 2025 | 07:00PM

Organizer: Silliman University Culture and Arts Council

Event Type: Classroom

Points: [1] Spectator

What

So What

Now What

The Unseen Flame was another performance we watched, held on September 27, 2025, at 7:00 PM. This one focused on Japanese culture with a mix of music, dance, and storytelling. It had that traditional vibe but also some modern touches, which made it really interesting to watch. My role was just being part of the audience, taking it all in.

It was honestly nice to see something different from the usual stuff we watch at school. I liked how it showed a lot of Japanese traditions and how detailed their performances are. I didn’t expect to enjoy it that much, but it was calming and kind of cool to see how much culture is packed into their art. The visuals and sounds were on point. I didn’t really learn a skill, but I did learn to appreciate cultural performances more. The hardest part was just staying focused the whole time since I zoned out a bit, not gonna lie, but overall it was relaxing and enjoyable.

The experience made me realize how diverse and creative different cultures can be. It’s not just about entertainment, it’s about expression and history too. I can apply that by being more open to watching and learning from other cultural events. I’d probably go again if they held something similar. The benefit of this kind of activity is that it helps you understand other cultures better and gives you a break from the usual academic grind. If I could do it again, I’d probably pay a bit more attention and maybe take some photos for memory’s sake.

Because of this activity, I am more appreciative of cultural performances and the effort that goes into making them.
